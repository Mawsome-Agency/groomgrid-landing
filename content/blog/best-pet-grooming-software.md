## What to Look for When Choosing Pet Grooming Software

Before choosing a platform, match your decision to your actual operation type:

**If you're a solo mobile groomer:** Prioritize native mobile app quality, offline capability, and a booking portal that works on your phone. A desktop-first platform is a daily friction tax.

**If you're a small salon (2–3 groomers):** Multi-staff calendar and tip tracking are non-negotiable. Make sure the platform doesn't charge per-seat fees that make it more expensive as you add groomers.

**If you're scaling beyond 5 groomers:** AI automation, rebooking tools, and lapsed client alerts pay real dividends at this scale. Staff scheduling optimization is worth paying for when you have 4+ people to coordinate.

**If budget is tight:** Understand what "free" or "$19/mo" tools actually limit. Feature locks on pet profiles, reminders, or reporting often create workarounds that cost more time than the savings on the subscription.

## Free vs. Paid Pet Grooming Software — Should You Start Free?

Free grooming tools typically monetize through per-booking transaction fees rather than a flat monthly subscription. At first glance, $0/month sounds better than $29/month. The math tells a different story.

At 25 bookings per week with a $75 average ticket and a 2% booking fee: that's $37.50 per week, or roughly $150 per month in fees. A $29/month paid plan costs 80% less.

Free makes sense only when you're in the first few weeks of your business — fewer than 15 bookings per week, actively testing the market. Once you're booking consistently, the transaction fee model becomes expensive fast.

## How to Switch Grooming Software Without Losing Your Client Data

Switching platforms is less complicated than it sounds if you plan it:

1. **Export your client records** from your current tool as a CSV — before you cancel the old plan
2. **Import into the new platform** — every reputable grooming software tool accepts CSV client imports
3. **Test with 5 real clients** before full cutover — verify appointment confirmation, reminders, and payment processing work as expected
4. **Communicate the change** to your regular clients — a simple text or email with your new booking link handles 80% of the transition
5. **Expect 4–6 weeks** for full client adoption of the new booking portal; keep a manual phone backup during the transition window

One important caveat: always verify data export capability before committing to any platform. Some tools make client export difficult deliberately. If a vendor can't tell you clearly how to export your client list, that's a red flag.
