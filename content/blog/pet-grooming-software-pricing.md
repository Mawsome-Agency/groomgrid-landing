# Pet Grooming Software Pricing: What to Expect, What to Avoid, and How to Choose

Pricing is confusing because vendors hide fees and use vague plan names. One platform advertises "$35/month" but requires five add-on modules at $25 each. Another shows a low price but requires an annual commitment — pay monthly and the price jumps 20%. Still others bury transaction fees in the fine print.

You need to understand total cost of ownership, not just the advertised monthly rate. This guide breaks down what you'll actually pay, which platforms hide the most fees, and how to calculate whether the software is worth it for your business.

## Watch Out for These Hidden Costs

Most vendors advertise their base price and hide the extras. These are the gotchas to watch for:

### Transaction Fees on Payments (0.5–3% per transaction)

Every grooming platform processes payments. Some use Stripe (transparent fees), others use proprietary systems (hidden fees). Transaction fees typically range from **0.5–3% per payment**.

Here's what this looks like in practice: If you charge $75 per grooming session and process 100 payments per month at 2.9% transaction fees, that's **$217.50/month in payment processing** — on top of your software subscription.

Some platforms build this into the subscription, others charge separately. Always ask: "What are the payment processing fees?" and factor them into your total cost.

### Per-Location Fees

Multi-location salons often pay more — sometimes significantly. Some platforms charge $20–$50/month per additional location. DaySmart Pet is known for per-location pricing that can quickly escalate.

If you're planning to expand to multiple locations, ask about location-based pricing before committing to a platform.

### Setup / Onboarding Fees

Some vendors charge $200–$500 for setup and onboarding. This was more common five years ago but still happens, especially with enterprise platforms. GroomGrid, MoeGo, and most modern competitors don't charge setup fees — but always verify.

### Add-On Module Fees

DaySmart Pet is the worst offender here. The base price might be $35/month, but you need add-on modules for almost every useful feature:
- +$25/month for marketing
- +$25/month for advanced reporting
- +$25/month for integrations
- +$25/month for client portal

By the time you've added the features you actually need, you're paying **$135+/month** — nearly 4x the advertised price.

### Annual vs. Monthly Rate Difference

Most platforms offer a discount for annual billing — typically **15–20%**. But if you pay monthly, you pay more. MoeGo also requires an annual commitment to access the lowest advertised price. Pay monthly and the price increases.

Here's the math: MoeGo Basic at $49/month annually equals $588/year. If MoeGo charges 20% more for monthly billing, that's $58.80/month or **$705.60/year** — a difference of **$117.60**.

## How to Calculate What Grooming Software Is Worth to You

Pricing only makes sense in context of revenue. The best way to think about it is ROI — return on investment.

**Revenue-based ROI calculation:**

If you groom 20 dogs/week at $75 per session, that's **$6,000/month in revenue**. A $49/month software subscription represents **0.8% of revenue**. If that software prevents even 2 no-shows per week ($150/month in saved revenue), you're already coming out ahead.

**No-show calculator:**

Most grooming software pays for itself by reducing no-shows. Automated SMS and email reminders can reduce no-shows by **30–50%**. Here's the math:

- 2 no-shows prevented per week × $75 per appointment = $150/week in saved revenue
- $150/week × 52 weeks = **$7,800/year in saved revenue**
- Software cost: $49/month × 12 = $588/year
- **Net ROI: $7,212/year**

Even if the software only prevents 1 no-show per week, that's **$3,600/year in saved revenue** vs. $588 in software costs. The software pays for itself 6x over.

**The breakeven reality:**

If your grooming software prevents **1 no-show every 2–3 weeks**, it has paid for itself. Most platforms (GroomGrid, MoeGo, Pawfinity) include automated reminders. The question isn't "can I afford software?" — it's "can I afford NOT to have software?"

## Is Free Pet Grooming Software Worth It?

GrooMore offers a free tier, and Groomsoft has a 30-day free trial. Free sounds appealing, especially for new businesses with zero revenue. But here's the reality:

**Free tier limitations:**

- No route optimization (critical for mobile groomers)
- Limited reminders (1–2 per week max)
- Restricted payment processing (you can't accept credit cards)
- Capped client count (usually 50–100 clients max)
- No AI features

**When free is OK:**

- You're brand new and haven't groomed any clients yet
- You're testing the grooming business idea before committing
- You only need a calendar for the first 1–2 months

**When free fails:**

- As soon as you need to accept credit card payments
- Once you exceed the client cap
- When route optimization becomes necessary
- The day you miss a revenue opportunity because the free tier blocked a feature

**The real cost of free:**

If the free tier causes you to miss 2 no-shows in a month ($150 in lost revenue) that could have been prevented with paid reminders, the "free" tier just cost you $150. The $20–$29/month that Groomsoft and GroomGrid charge is insurance against lost revenue.
